Shown is a visual representation of the phenomenon known as illusory pausing. This was designed as a project for my Psychology of Perception class. Goldberg & Pomerantz discovered that when dots move towards each other at a constant velocity, we briefly perceive that the dots pause when they meet and appear to merge into one dot. The optimal settings at which people can see this illusion varies depending the speed, size, and opacity of the dots, so settings have been made available for the user to adjust.
